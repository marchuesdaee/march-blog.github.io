Experiencing Cordova RORO's Rich Biodiversity

RORO

ENVIRONMENTAL

Roro Port evolved became an essential route for business and tourists. Fresh food was put onto trucks by farmers, and families were leaving for far-off islands on vacations. The steady murmur of the port beat in time with Cordova's heartbeat, converting the peaceful village into a bustling entrance.

With a large number of automobiles and trucks waiting to board the boats, Cordova port can be crowded and dirty. There will smells, possibly some vehicle fumes and salty air. It's wonderful to see the boats come and leave, but it's noisy with people talking and engines running. It can get windy at the edges, so use caution!
